The Coastal Challenge

Understanding the growing threat to West Africa's coastline

West Africa's coastline, home to over 105 million people, faces increasing threats from coastal flooding due to climate change, sea-level rise, and extreme weather events. Coastal communities in countries such as Senegal, Ghana, and Nigeria are particularly vulnerable.

Coastal degradation in West Africa is causing rising economic and human losses. In 2017 alone, four countries – Benin, Côte d'Ivoire, Senegal and Togo – incurred damages of $3.8 billion, representing 5.3% of their combined GDP. Coastal hazards were responsible for over 13,000 deaths. Driven by climate change, urbanization and environmental degradation, flooding across the region is becoming more frequent and severe, exposing growing vulnerabilities in coastal resilience.

Without effective warning systems, these communities have limited time to prepare for flooding events, resulting in significant loss of life, displacement, and economic damage.

Our Innovative Solution

Developing a comprehensive coast-flooding early warning system for West African coastal communities

Advanced Monitoring

We implement a multi-source coastal monitoring framework that integrates earth observation data(satellite, models and in situ) with hindcast marine datasets from Copernicus (waves and sea level anomalies) and FES tide models to compute Total Water Level (TWL). Using statistical analysis in Python and R, this system supports long-term characterization of extreme coastal events and threshold-based early warning triggers.

Prediction Models

Machine learning models are under development to analyse historical hydrodynamic and atmospheric data, enabling probabilistic forecasting of extreme coastal events and enhancing the precision of early warning alerts across the Gulf of Guinea.

Multi-Channel Alerts

Instant alerts via SMS, mobile apps, community sirens, and radio broadcasts to ensure warnings reach everyone in at-risk areas.

System Overview

How our early warning system protects coastal communities

Comprehensive Flood Monitoring Network

Integrates real-time data and advanced modeling to provide accurate forecasts of coastal flooding events up to 72 hours in advance.

Delivers timely alerts through multiple communication channels to reach all relevant stakeholders.

Empowers local governments and emergency responders to implement proactive disaster management strategies.

Enables vulnerable communities to prepare and respond effectively, reducing loss of life and property damage.

Supports long-term resilience by informing planning and risk reduction policies across the Gulf of Guinea.
